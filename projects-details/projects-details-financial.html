FINANCIAL AUTONOMY OF MARGINALIZED COMMUNITY

IMHH continues to pledge to help building health and hope within vulnerable people (marginalized people) since it has been created. In partnership with Center for Health and Hope, IMHH has done a lot of work that has helped the vulnerable people, and is still helping them.

Iclusive salon was initiated in 2019 to support the sexual minorities around the City of Gisenyi. The inclusive mission for health and hope (IMHH) has been at its best again when it comes to financial autonomy for communities in Rwanda.


Whit this project, the sexual minorities have been able to earn a living by themselves (self-reliance) unlike the old days where they would only depend on others, this has therefore helped them to have hope in them and showing them that the way they were born it is not their choice. IMHHH understood that the diversity is the that can develop the society once it is well exploited.
